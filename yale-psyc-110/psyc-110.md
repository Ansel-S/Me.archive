## Foundations

### The Neuron

As Lewis Carroll's Alice might have phrased it,
> You're nothing but a pack of neurons.

**All-or-None Law**: Much like binary code in computing, a neuron either fires a full electrical impulse or it doesn't fire at all.

**Neurotransmitters**: Chemical messengers released into the synapse to communicate between cells.

### Brain Structure

- **Subcortical Structures**: Basic instincts
- **The Cerebal Cortex**: (*E.g. reasoning, language, perception*)
- **Hemispheric Specialization**: The left brain focuses on logic and language, while the right brain focuses on spatial and holistic perception.
- **Split-brain**: Visual field and movements are not coordinated.

## Foundations: Freud

### 1. The Core Conflict: Structure of Mind
Freud believed personality is a constant battle between three forces driven by **Unconscious Motivation**.

* **Id (The Child):** Driven by the **Pleasure Principle**. Seeks immediate gratification (hunger, sex, aggression).
* **Superego (The Judge):** The internal moral compass. Learned from parents/society; focuses on how we *should* behave.
* **Ego (The Mediator):** Driven by the **Reality Principle**. It tries to satisfy the Id’s desires in a way that is realistic and acceptable to the Superego.


### 2. Psychosexual Stages of Development
Freud argued that personality is shaped by how we handle "sexual" energy in childhood.

1.  **Oral Stage:** Focus on the mouth (sucking, eating).
2.  **Anal Stage:** Focus on control and cleanliness (toilet training).
3.  **Phallic Stage:** Focus on genitals and sexual identity (Oedipus complex).
    * *Note: This is where "Penis Envy" occurs in girls.*
4.  **Latency Stage:** A quiet period; sexual drives are repressed/hidden.
5.  **Genital Stage:** The final stage (puberty onwards); mature adult sexuality.


### 3. Defense Mechanisms
The Ego uses these "tricks" to reduce anxiety caused by the Id and Superego.

| Mechanism | Definition | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Displacement** | Shifting impulses to a safer, weaker target. | A boy is mad at his father but kicks his dog instead. |
| **Projection** | Attributing your own unwanted thoughts to others. | A man with sexual urges asks a woman, "Why are you thinking about sex with me?" |
| **Rationalization** | Making up logical excuses for bad behavior. | Parents hit a child and claim, "It’s for the child's own good." |
| **Regression** | Retreating to a more childish behavior. | An adult throwing a tantrum when they don't get their way. |

### 4. Scientific Criticism
Modern psychology is often critical of Freud for several reasons:
* **Penis Envy:** Viewed as sexist and outdated.
* **Unfalsifiability:** His theories are impossible to test, prove, or disprove. If a theory cannot be tested, it is not considered "scientific."
* **The Legacy:** Despite the flaws, he correctly identified that the **unconscious mind** and **childhood experiences** are incredibly powerful.
